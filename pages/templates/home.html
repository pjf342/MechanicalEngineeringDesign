Strength and Cold Work

Cold working a material will change its strengths. A new yield strength will be produced, as well as a new ultimate tensile strength due to a reduction in area.

Some examples of a cold working process are extrusions through a die, shearing a material, and running a material through two rollers.

2D Principal Stresses

A 2D element can experience stresses (sigma) and shear stresses (tau).

At a certain orientation, the element can experience maximum stresses and shear stresses.

This calculator will be able to provide the average normal stress, the principal stresses (sigma 1 and sigma 2) and the angle they occur at, and the maximum shear stress (tau) and the angle it occurs at.

Cylindrical Pressure Vessel Stresses

The pressure vessel can be capped or uncapped. Stresses can be calculated on the inner or outer walls.

A carbonated drink is an example of a capped/closed pressure vessel, while a gun barrel is an example of an uncapped/open pressure vessel.

If the vessel is closed/capped, there will be an axial stress.
